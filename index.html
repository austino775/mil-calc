How to Use Mil‑Calc

Mil‑Calc helps you estimate the distance to a target using your scope to measure a target of known size.

Step 1: Choose a Known Target Size

Pick a target in which you know the size of.
⚠️ Accuracy here is critical.
If your target size is off, your distance calculation will also be off. Even a small error can result in a significant distance difference.

Step 2: Measure Through Your Scope

Look through your scope and measure how many MILs or MOA the target spans across your scope's reticle.

  • Confirm your scope's reticle is MOA or MIL
  • Confirm your app's settings reflect MIL or MOA
  • Measure carefully
  • Use decimal precision when possible (e.g. 2.4 MIL, not 2 MIL)

The more precise your measurement, the better your result.

Step 3: Enter the Values

Enter:
• The known target size (The actual measurement of the target's size)
• The measured MIL or MOA value (What you saw through your scope)

Then tap Calculate.
The app will display a calculated distance to the target based on your inputs.

To calculate a second time or another target's distance select 'Mil Another Target'.

Using the Reference Section

The Reference section provides common average and standardized sizes to help you when you do not know a target's exact dimensions.

  • IPSC target measurements
  • Various animal sizes
  • Standardized objects like building materials and traffic signs

For animal references, measurements are typically listed as Vital Zone Height. Some small animals, like prairie dogs, use Standing Height. To add a reference measurement to your distance calculation, select “Add Selected Size”.

Please note: These size measurements are approximations, but often close enough to be useful.

Important Notes

  • This app provides calculated distances based on your inputs
  • Always confirm distance when safety is a concern
  • The better your input, the better your output
